History

LEGO's history began in the 1930s when Ole Kirk Christiansen founded the company in Denmark,
initially producing wooden toys. By 1947, LEGO shifted to plastic, and in 1958,
it revolutionized the toy industry with the patented interlocking brick design.
The 1960s saw LEGO expanding with themed sets and the opening of the first LEGOLAND park in 1968.
In the 1980s and 1990s, LEGO diversified its offerings with themes like space and pirates, and introduced LEGO Technic and LEGO Mindstorms, blending building with robotics.
The 2000s marked a period of innovation with popular franchises such as Star Wars and LEGO Architecture,and the company expanded into digital play.
In the 2020s, LEGO focused on sustainability with products made from recycled materials and continued to grow its global presence and digital footprint, solidifying its role as a leader in creative play and innovation.
